Classes vs Modules

01/24/2015

The premise behind Object-Oriented Programming is that concepts and processes are defined as classes from which objects are created. In my last post, I explained it through the example of the class Player. I was able to create multiple instances of players, or Player objects, with their own attributes and action capabilities through methods.

Modules on the other hand, provide a means to hold classes, methods and constants in a single defined space. They provide ways to organize methods, classes, and constants into separate namespaces. Functionality can also be shared between disparate classes. Modules can be includes into other classes, giving them access to methods within the module.







Modules are included in certain Kernel classes such as Array to provide access to methods. For example, the Array class contains about 20 counting and iteration related methods such as #collect, and #sort, which are provided by the Enumerable module.
